Customs Duty Policy

Without exception, all international postal items undergo customs clearance in the recipient's country. They are checked for prohibited items and for the weight and value of the goods being shipped. Each country sets its own rules for postal individuals. duty-free limit. This is the threshold for duty-free import of goods by international mail. Payment of taxes and duties shall be borne by the recipient of the goods. Payments are charged only for the amount exceeding the duty-free limit, and not for the total cost of the parcel. Before placing an order, check the exact customs limits with the customs office of your country. Below is the data for 2019..

European Union The common customs area is an integral part of the European Union. However, despite the uniform customs rules, each country has its own characteristics, namely: the rate of value added tax (VAT) and the cost of customs services.
From July 1, 2021
Cancellation of the current threshold for tax-free import of goods (now 22 euros) when importing to EU countries. The change will lead to the need for formal customs declaration of all goods imported into the EU countries and the collection of VAT. Resetting VAT De Minimis will affect all shipments imported into the EU from the rest of the world. As part of these changes, the customs authorities are increasing the requirements for the provision of complete and accurate information for customs declaration. This will improve the effectiveness of security screening and help combat piracy, counterfeiting and unfair competition, and ensure full collection of taxes and duties.
